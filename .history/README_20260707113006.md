
# Research Platform Engineering: A Reference Lab

An open, reproducible blueprint for building a university-style research
computing platform as code. HPC scheduling, monitoring, Kubernetes,
network fabric, firewall segmentation, and backup infrastructure, all
provisioned with OpenTofu, configured with Ansible, and validated by CI
on every push.

The goal is educational: to document, in working code, the patterns used
in production research computing (the platforms behind HPC clusters and
research clouds at universities), at a scale anyone can reproduce on a
single machine.

## Who this is for

- Engineers moving from systems administration into platform engineering
- HPC and research computing staff adopting IaC, CI/CD, and modern
  monitoring
- Teams outgrowing Vagrant/local-VM testing workflows and looking for
  ephemeral, API-driven test environments on Proxmox or similar platforms
- Anyone managing a brownfield estate and planning migrations (Nagios to
  Prometheus, Puppet to Ansible, manual provisioning to IaC) without
  downtime or disruption

## What this covers

- **Ephemeral infrastructure testing:** spin up a full platform stack
  from a branch, test changes, tear it down. No permanent staging VMs,
  no RAM ceiling on a workstation, no manual branch checkouts.
- **Monitoring migration:** Nagios and Prometheus running side by side
  against the same cluster, with a documented, staged cutover path.
  Because real migrations are coexistence periods, not cutovers.
- **Configuration management interoperability:** Ansible-first, with
  patterns for coexisting with Puppet-managed estates during transition.
- **HPC scheduling and GPU workloads:** Slurm cluster with GRES GPU
  scheduling, MPI, and researcher-facing workflows.
- **Multi-DC and network fabric patterns:** leaf-spine BGP fabric,
  firewall segmentation between management and compute networks,
  modelling the split most university datacentres operate.
- **Backup, restore, and disaster recovery:** scheduled backups with
  tested restores and written runbooks, not just backup jobs that
  have never been verified.
- **Cost recovery and service management:** documentation patterns for
  charging models and service catalogues relevant to shared research
  computing platforms.

## Design principles

1. Everything as code. No manual steps after the Proxmox hypervisor
   install.
2. Production patterns, miniature scale. Runs on a single node.
3. Migration over greenfield. Includes legacy coexistence exercises
   because real estates are brownfield.
4. Ephemeral over permanent. Test environments are created and destroyed
   by CI, not maintained by hand.
5. Observable from birth. Every VM is monitored from the moment it
   exists.

## Proxmox node specification

- Dell Precision T1700
- Intel Core i7-4790 @ 3.60GHz, 4 cores / 8 threads, VT-x/VT-d
- 32 GB RAM, 2 TB storage (ZFS)
- GTX 1050 Ti (GPU passthrough for GRES scheduling and inference workloads)
- 3x 1GbE NICs (management, compute, fabric lab)

## Reproducing

```bash
git clone https://github.com/harunaadoga/devops-iac-infra.git
cd devops-iac-infra/tofu/foundation
export TF_VAR_pve_api_token='<your-proxmox-api-token>'
tofu init && tofu apply
```

Three Rocky Linux 9 VMs, configured and SSH-ready, in under a minute.
`tofu destroy` removes everything. The infrastructure is the repo.

## Contributing

running research computing platforms in production. If a pattern here
differs from what works in your estate, open an issue: the comparison is
the learning.

Specific areas where production experience would strengthen the project:

- Monitoring migration patterns (especially Nagios to Prometheus at scale)
- Configuration management coexistence (Puppet and Ansible in the same estate)
- Ephemeral test environment workflows beyond Vagrant

- Network fabric automation in research computing datacentres
